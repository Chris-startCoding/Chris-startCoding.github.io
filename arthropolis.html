Final Bachelor Project

Arthropolis

What? An interactive educational tool which makes children aged 10-12 aware of the urgency of incorporating insects into our diet.

Why? Insects are a very sustainable and healthy food source. Especially when they are used as a meat-substitute. Incorporating them into Western diets however, is a process that involves many stakeholders and long-term actions. Including increasing availability, breaking down negative associations of insects and more awareness about the benefits in health and sustainability. By letting children experience this process from a birdâ€™s-eye view, they become aware of the importance of eating insects and the obstacles that come with it.

How? The child plays as the mayor of Arthropolis. Players perform actions like changing meat farms to insect farms, opening insect restaurants and imposing a meat tax. How their actions affect insect and meat consumption, energy use, greenhouse gas emissions and water use is displayed on the bottom of the board.
